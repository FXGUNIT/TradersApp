# Army Tone Guide for Traders Regiment

- Use short, direct sentences. Clarity over flourish.
- Phrases: Welcome, recruit. You’re in the Regiment now. You’ll hear from the Chief within 48 hours. Stand by for orders.
- Avoid casual slang; favor concise commands and decisive tone.
- Emails should be brief, with a single call to action.
- UI copy should reflect alertness and discipline (e.g., “Action required” vs. “Please”).

Sample copy (Invite email):
- Subject: Welcome to Traders Regiment – Invitation Enlisted
- Body: Welcome to the Regiment, recruit. You’ve earned a place in our operation. Your invite is recorded. The Chief will verify your identity in the next 48 hours. You’ll receive a welcome email if you’re cleared. Stand by for orders.

Sample copy (Welcome email):
- Subject: Welcome Aboard, Recruit
- Body: You are cleared for duty. The Regiment stands with you. Expect your official welcome message shortly. Move with purpose.

This guide should help maintain a consistent tone across invites, alerts, and onboarding communications.
